buck circuits


When the switching tube Q is controlled by the driving pulse to conduct, the renewal diode D is cut off and the input power supply is connected to the energy storage inductor L. Therefore, the input-output voltage difference Ui−UoU_i-U_oUi​−Uo​ is added to L, so that the current through L gradually increases. At this stage, in addition to supplying power to the load, a portion of the power is stored in inductor L and capacitor C.

When the switch is disconnected, L is disconnected from the power input, but since the inductor current cannot change abruptly in an instant, a reverse electric potential is generated on the inductor L to maintain the current through it constant. At this point, the current-continuity diode D turns on, and the power stored in the inductor L is supplied to the load through the circuit formed by D. (mainly the capacitor C to the load power supply, this stage has been to charge the capacitor to maintain voltage stability)

boost circuit


When the switch is closed, the diode D cuts off and the input voltage returns through the inductor L. The current through the inductor increases linearly. At this time, the output filter capacitor discharges to the load.
When the switch is off, the reverse electric potential VLV_LVL​ is generated on the inductor L to maintain the through current constant because the inductor current cannot change abruptly in an instant. At this time, the diode D is on, and the input voltage and the two voltages of the inductor are connected in series to supply the load with a voltage exceeding the input size and to charge the output filter capacitor.

Losses of DCDC

  1. Switching tube loss: This is the main loss of the switching power supply, mainly including switching loss, conduction loss. Therefore, you should try to choose a relatively small on-resistance switching tube as the core components of the switching power supply.
  2. inductance-capacitance loss: inductance loss mainly includes DC resistance loss, capacitance loss mainly includes leakage current loss. Therefore, you should try to choose a smaller DC resistance of the inductor and a smaller leakage current capacitive components.
  3. diode loss: mainly includes conduction loss and switching loss. Therefore, you should try to choose a diode with a small on-state voltage drop and a short reverse recovery time, such as Schottky diodes or fast recovery diodes.

Ripple of DCDC circuit

The actual output voltage ripple is determined by the inductor current and output impedance, and consists of three parts

  1. the voltage drop formed by the inductor current ripple through the parasitic resistance ESR of the output capacitor
  2. charging and discharging of the output capacitor
  3. the voltage burst caused by the parasitic inductor

Ripple generally has the following types.

  1. High frequency ripple. Switching power supply switching tube in the on and off, there will be a switch in the circuit with the same frequency as the rise and fall time or an odd number of times the frequency of the noise, generally tens of MHz. the same diode in the reverse recovery moment, the equivalent circuit for the resistor-capacitor and inductor series, will cause resonance, the noise generated by the frequency of tens of MHz. there are high-frequency transformer leakage will also produce high-frequency interference. These noises are generally called high-frequency ripple noise, the amplitude is usually much larger than the ripple. 2.
  2. noise generated by switching devices. As the switch is switched on and off, the current in the inductor L is also fluctuating up and down in the effective value of the output current. Therefore, a ripple of the same frequency as the switch also appears at the output.
  3. adjust the ripple noise caused by the control loop. The actual circuit control loop to have a time response, can not achieve linear regulation, so the output voltage will be instantaneously high and low, and may even cause the power system oscillation, resulting in ripple noise.

To reduce the output voltage ripple can be done by reducing the inductor current ripple or by reducing the output capacitor impedance.
The inductor current ripple is inversely proportional to the switching frequency and inductance when the input and output voltages and load are constant. Increasing the inductance or switching frequency can effectively reduce the output voltage ripple, but the switching frequency and inductance are often limited by the circuit efficiency and size.
If a suitable capacitor is still not enough to meet the ripple requirement, a second LC filter can be added to further reduce the output voltage ripple.

DCDC operation mode

CCM operation mode

CCM (ContinuousConduction Mode): In a switching cycle, the inductor current never goes to 0. Or the inductor never "resets", meaning that the inductor flux never returns to 0 during the switching cycle, and there is still current flowing in the coil when the power tube is closed.

The waveform of buck converter in CCM operation mode

  1. Advantages
    The primary peak current is relatively small, but will superimpose a large DC component; duty cycle is not related to the output current size, so it is suitable for applications where the load current varies greatly; the capacitance requirement for the secondary output is relatively low, which is conducive to reducing the capacitor capacity and volume.
  2. Disadvantages
    The secondary rectifier diode has the problem of reverse recovery, which causes heat and EMI problems; feedback compensation is complicated, there is a right half-zero problem; requires a larger core and more primary turns.

DCM operating mode

DCM, (Discontinuous Conduction Mode): In the switching cycle, the inductor current will always go to 0, meaning that the inductor is properly "reset", i.e. the inductor current is zero when the power switch is closed.

The waveform of buck converter in DCM operation mode

  1. Advantages
    Because the secondary rectifier diode is turned off before the primary switch turns on, there is no reverse recovery problem; feedback compensation is easy, there is no right half-face zero problem, so the transient response caused by sudden changes in load current is faster, the dynamics is good, and the overshoot is not too high.
  2. Disadvantages
    All power components to withstand the peak current is relatively large, the effective value of the current is also large, to a certain extent will affect the efficiency of the circuit; large di / dt will bring EMI problems; because the duty cycle and the output current size, to get a stable output, there must be a minimum load problem; the secondary output capacitance requirements are also higher, otherwise there will be a big ripple problem.
