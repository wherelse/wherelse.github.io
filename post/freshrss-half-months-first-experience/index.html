RSS is a long-established way of reading, but it's gradually declining, and now that recommendation algorithms are the norm, it's becoming difficult to get back to the basics of RSS subscriptions. In the domestic Internet environment, each platform is constantly pursuing private domain traffic, resulting in a large amount of quality content being bound within a small environment, out of reach of many people. In this environment, why did I start to try RSS?

First is the RSS way of reading, reading environment is pure, not disturbed by other factors, also will not be like the cell phone APP to see a thing when looking at the distraction by other things, and keep brushing, can effectively improve the reading efficiency and memory depth. The second is the use of RSS way to read, the content is completely in accordance with their own preferences to add, at the same time because of its regular update state, will not be wrong unread content, like the video of B station, some like watching the video, temporarily no time to watch, will be a large number of other videos flooded down, and then forgotten, and RSS reader support has read unread filtering, to avoid content omission. Lastly, I think RSS is the best way to read blog posts and public articles. Individual independent blogs and publics have high uncertainty about the update time and period, so using RSS can efficiently read new blog posts and not miss them.

After deciding to start using RSS, after some screening, I finally chose FreshRSS, which is simple to build, easy to build using Docker, while the interface is more modern and at the same time fully functional. FreshRSS also supports multiple users, so if you want an account to experience it, you can leave a message and I can send you an account to experience it. In half a month of use, the biggest feeling is still the immersive reading experience, you can focus on reading the content of the article, greatly improving the reading experience.

However, there are some disadvantages.

  1. because of the traffic, many sites do not support RSS feeds, and can not find a subscription source.
  2. quality subscription sources/websites take time to find, which is somewhat time-consuming and difficult to obtain widely.
  3. There are no good domestic RSS subscription services, and foreign services are difficult to access, so you must build and maintain your own if you want to use them.

Finally, although RSS is becoming more and more niche, but there are still many people love this way of reading, will certainly always exist, if you see this article, also feel like, welcome to join. Also recommended are a few ways to get RSS feeds.
